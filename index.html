The roads I have traveled

A short biography about some of the things I have seen. May 13,2019

So I am Software Engineer that has been on an interesting career path. After graduating from the University of Idaho in 1999, I started off my career at Sun Microsystems, Inc. My first job was working on a replacement for a service request ticketing system. The project utilized custom web application framework built by Siebel Systems. After a year or so Siebel scrapped the their web application to take a new approach which essentially tossed out numerous hours of developer work.

An opportunity came up within Sun IT to work on an internal project which gave employees access to their work calendars and email at home. How exciting was this! Now remember this was in 2000/2001 The internet was just starting to get very interesting in that content was no longer so static and use of the <blink&rt; tag was declining. Gone were the days of looking at web sites with gopher and dynamic sites were starting to appear on the scene utilizing Adobe Flash player. The work was interesting and eventually the core framework was spun off as the Sun Portal Server. After awhile I wanted to try something new.

I next went to Peoplesoft to work with their portal group. My stint was short in part because the place seemed chaotic and I just didn't really feel like a good fit there. A friend told me about a job with Maxtor Corporation which I jumped at. Maxtor was a fun job that ended too quickly due to an acquisition by Seagate Corp. I was given freedom to drive implementation of the Java development environment. I wrote a couple of small JSP based apps and eventually ended up taking over development responsibilities for www.maxtor.com. The site utilized Vignette's content manager and JSP tags for grabbing the content. Once news of the acquisition hit I was offered an incentive to stay until the company was shut down. Since the amount of new content getting published on the web site was lessened the system was quite stable. Eventually we got bored and would fill the slack time with ping pong, lunch time sand volleyball games and the Friday afternoon round of golf with my manager.

When it was time I went back to Sun Microsystems to work on Sun One Portal Server. I worked on numerous JSR 168 portlets and things were going well until the layoff. This was during 2007-2008 when the Great Recesion hit. No one was purchasing anything and the company killed the product to cut costs since it had never been a high revenue generating application. I was laid off for about 5 months but got hired again by Sun Microsystems to work on the Ops Center product. Initially it started off as a competitor to VirtualBox but it evolved in software to manage virtualization software for the Sparc OS. I worked primarily around the virtual network features doing user interface work using Dojo, ExtJS with Java and Spring used in the middle layer. The product seemed to be doing ok given the business environment but then word came that Oracle Corporation was looking to acquire Sun Micro. Those of you who remember their history know that eventually Oracle was successful. For awhile we continued to work on Ops Center but then we got news that we would be re-organized into Oracle's Enterprise Manager on the user interface team.

The change was interesting. Oracle being the large software company that they are had a lot of processes in place that were not used by the Sun Microsystems group. It took a bit of change but it didn't take long to see the value in most of these processes and policies. I did this work for 2-3 years and then our group was moved of the Enterprise Manager and moved on to the Oracle Cloud. I eventually ended up taking ownership for the several visualizations. Our product group was responsible for Target Analytics and our primary interface was the Universal Data Explorer (UDE). The UDE would take various criterion based on metrics to filter the data set. Then the visualization could render a text display using the criteria metadata or numerous graphs including Gauge, Histogram, Bar and Line charts. The work was interesting and could be quite challenging at times. I think the challenge was what I liked most about the job. Unfortunately due to a company acquisition and some internal politics a fairly major reduction in force occurred and I have again been thrust into the job market to find my new home.
